About

Enrique acebo is a PhD student in business economics at University of León in Spain. In his research, he studies how firms interact with their ecosystems leverage new technologies for innovation, value creation and competitive advantage. His work explores how the different stakeholders of the innovation ecosystem interact between them and ways for managers to get the most of it. It thereby sheds light on the interaction of different agents in innovation systems and the resulting implications for economic growth and environmental sustainability.

To study the determinants of firm innovation activities and performance, his research builds on ideas from a range of disciplines including economics, business strategy and economic geography. Furthermore, it employs a variety of methods from econometrics and the field of causal inference. Enrique Acebos’s work provides insights for managers on how increase their results collaborating with differnt types of stakeholders. And, for policymakers on optimally designing public R&D support schemes.

In 2018, he was awardaded with one of the 17 FPU positions available that year in Spain for doctoral studies on Business and Economics. FPU fellowships are awarded on merit of academic achievements and are highly competitive. As part of the FPU program, he teaches practices lessons in "Innovation Management" and "Management Foundations". He is going to vist the department of Strategy and Innovation of the Copenhague Business School in Denmark as a visiting PhD student in the first quarter of 2022.

His work has been published in Business Strategy and the Enviroment, Science and Public Policy and European journal of Innovation Management. And presented in international conferences such as DRUID and ACEDE.
