About

My research focuses on innovation ecosystems. Throughout my research, I investigate how firms interact with the other crucial agents of the innovation ecosystem to leverage new technologies, value creation and competitive advantages.

I am a third-year PhD student in business economics in the University of León (Spain). To study innovation ecosystems, I use ideas from various disciplines,including economics, and business strategy. Furthermore, I employ a variety of methods from econometrics and the field of causal inference. My work provides insights for managers and policy makers.

In 2018, I was awarded one of the seventeen FPU predoctoral fellowships available in Spain for doctoral studies in Business and Economics. As part of the FPU program, I teach practice lessons in "Innovation Management" and "Management Foundations". During, the first quarter of 2022, I am visiting the Department of Strategy and Innovation of the Copenhague Business School.

My work has been published in well-known journals like Business Strategy and the Environment, Science and Public Policy and European Journal of Innovation Management and presented in prestigious international conferences such as DRUID and ACEDE.
